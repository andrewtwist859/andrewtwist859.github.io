Support to study at Sheffield

From contextual offers to a dedicated team. Find support tailored to you.

Access+

Make sure you check whether you meet our Access+ criteria. If you live in a certain postcode or were eligible for free school meals (find more criteria and info on our website), you could get:

  • A contextual offer (up to a two A Level grade or equivalent reduction)
  • Bursaries for travel to campus and essential IT equipment
  • A dedicated support team and online help and guidance sessions
  • The opportunity to apply for a mentor to help you transition to university

Disrupted studies

If your GCSE or post-16 studies were affected by a personal, social or home issue, tell us using our disrupted studies form, to help us consider your application.

Alternative entry options to study

  • A foundation year is a great option if you don’t meet our standard entry requirements.
  • You could earn while you learn, gaining skills, qualifications and work experience through an apprenticeship in healthcare or engineering at Sheffield. Search apprenticeships on our website.
  • If you’re a mature student, you don’t need standard qualifications - we’ll take your experience into account.
  • If you’re an international student and your high school qualifications aren’t accepted for direct entry, the University of Sheffield International College offers pathway programmes to university study.
