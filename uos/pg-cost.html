Cost of living

A city you can afford to enjoy

Sheffield is one of the UK’s most affordable cities. From parks and galleries, to job opportunities that fit around your studies, and countless discounts, this is the place to live and study affordably.

Rent

Average monthly room rents vary across the city. As a guide, an en-suite room in University accommodation with all bills included is around £682 a month.

Travel

Get around, about and out to the Peak District for less than the price of a coffee.

As a student, you can get discount travel for both tram and buses in South Yorkshire.

Sheffield is walkable with a variety of cycling routes available both in and around the city.

How I save and spend money as a postgraduate student

I chose to study in Sheffield because of the new opportunities my course opens up in the legal industry. My course is one of the very few to allow non-law students to do an MA instead of a PGDL course.

I am a self-funded postgraduate student. I was eligible for the masters student loan from student finance as I am an international student on home fee status.

I work as a student ambassador for widening participation. The role allows me to work flexibly around my studies.

Because I live in University accommodation in Ranmoor, I take advantage of the free weekly events held by the Residence Life team. They host a range of events such as T-shirt painting, film screenings, meals and other wellbeing events. They also offer limited free tickets for events. This year I was able to get free tickets to the Lunar and Chinese New Year Gala and the International Cultural Evenings, an annual event in the Students’ Union, through the Residence Life offer.

To save money, I use the community fridges in both student accommodation and the Students’ Union. The community fridge is a place where you share unwanted or surplus food, so it’s great to pick up items that would otherwise have gone to waste.

Yi Wang
