Learning patterns designed around you

Your strength and your ambitions. Your passions and your concerns. They form the foundations of our teaching.

Study hours and how you’re assessed will depend on your chosen course. However, every student can expect a cohesive learning programme — combining modules and projects to support the future you have in mind.

What you learn is directly influenced by our internationally excellent research. When you come to Sheffield you’ll see exactly how research-led teaching takes things to a whole new level. Because the people leading the award-winning breakthroughs, the discoveries and new ideas are the people teaching you, every single day. You’re part of the next generation of professionals who will change society for the better.

Research at Sheffield isn’t just a means to an end. This questioning spirit, the need to understand, is also a state of mind — a way of seeing the world that will enrich your life for years to come.

You’ll be taught and supervised by world-leading researchers, and experts in their field. And they’ll pass their knowledge, their enthusiasm and their professional standards, to you.

A day in the life of a postgraduate student

When I came for my interview, I found everyone to be extremely friendly. The city is the perfect size with a great mixture of greenery and city life. The facilities in the University are also brilliant.

7.30am: I wake up and tidy the house. Then I have breakfast and make a study plan for the day.

10.30am: I head to the Information Commons (library) to catch up on work and study. The good thing about the University is the amount of study spaces it has. I’m a big fan of the Information Commons, especially the silent study space on Floor 2. It’s nice, spacious, bright, airy and quiet, which is the most important thing.

12.30pm: I head to the Students’ Union for lunch. There’s great options if you want to buy food plus a microwave so you can heat up leftovers too! Coffee Revolution is my favourite part of the SU. It has a nice buzz and good coffee. The Swap Shop also has some gems, I have picked up some good books there! Plus, all the societies are very welcoming — I’m part of MedSoc and the Irish Society.

2.00 - 5.00pm: I normally spend my afternoons in lectures. Physician Associate Studies is a bit different to other postgraduate courses. It’s a two year intensive course to prepare us for life in the NHS. We also have anatomy lab sessions and weekly small group sessions with our tutor going over important topics. Every month we have clinical seminars where a patient comes in and tells us about their life and conditions.

7.00pm: Like many other students, I love going to Bolehills Park in Crookes. I’ll meet friends to watch the sunset. One good thing about all the hills is that the views go on for miles! I also have three part-time jobs alongside studying a full-time masters. I am an anatomy demonstrator for the University helping with the education of undergraduate Bioscience students. I’m also a student ambassador and have lots of babysitting gigs!”

Áine Kearins
Physician Associate Studies MMedSci
