How do I live my best student life on a budget?

It’s easy to live on a student budget in Sheffield with:

  1. Affordable rent

  2. Paid work as an Ambassador for the University

  3. Student discounts on nights out, food and drink

  4. Budget-friendly supermarkets

  5. Part-time jobs in the city

Eleanor Moorton

Q&A: My placement year at Jaguar Land Rover UK

How did the University support your placement search?

The placement team was instrumental, offering weekly job listings and refining my applications. It helped me secure an incredible opportunity.

Can you tell us about your placement?

I worked at Jaguar Land Rover UK, contributing to groundbreaking projects in the Chassis, ADAS, and Battery departments.

What were your highlights?

Working on developing control architectures for future technologies like the 2028 Range Rover and performing Failure Injection Testing for lane-centring features.

What difference has the experience made?

It has shaped my career ambitions and confidence. I’ve boosted my technical and problem-solving skills, preparing me for a career in innovative engineering.

Top 3 differences between school and uni

  1. Uni work is a lot more independent and you dive deeper into the subjects that you love. I can personalise my assignments and research to focus on my interests!

  2. Even though it’s more independent, you can easily access one-to-one support. I’ve built a network of support with academic staff who help with all aspects of my learning.

  3. Uni teaching can be more accessible for disabled and neurodiverse students. I can study in more diverse and unique ways.

My year abroad in 100 words

I had the opportunity to spend a year abroad during my studies at Sheffield. I split my year in two, spending the first semester studying at the University of Valencia, Spain, and the second semester working for an accountancy firm in Lyon, France.

The experience gave me an insight into two different cultures and the opportunity to work in a total of four different languages. The year abroad was a testing yet incredibly rewarding experience. I learnt so much, not only about other ways of life, but also about myself. Plus, I made so many new friends along the way!

5 things you need to know about your SU

  1. Our Students’ Union (SU) is an independent charity, run by student officers elected by students. You’ll automatically become a member when you arrive.

  2. Our SU is your place to get support, discover something new or make a change in the world.

  3. You’ll find over 300 student-led societies - more than 11,000 students were members of our societies in 2024! Across the SU, there are loads of students working to represent your views and lived experiences.

  4. Your SU is home to lots of places to eat and drink, support services and venues which host weekly nights out, live music, comedy and unique one-off events. We host cultural celebrations and global food festivals, so you can explore the world from within our SU.

  5. We have a zero-waste store and a clothes swap shop, where you can refill your groceries or refresh your wardrobe to help to reduce waste on campus.
