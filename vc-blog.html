The value of international students to the UK

Professor Alan Anywhere | 23 January 2023

As the Vice Chancellor of the University of Anywhere, I have had the pleasure of welcoming students from all over the world to our campus. These international students bring with them a wealth of cultural diversity and perspectives, enriching the educational experience for everyone at our university.

But the benefits of international students extend far beyond just our campus. In fact, international students are a vital part of the UK's economy and society.

First and foremost, international students make a significant economic contribution to the UK. According to a report by Universities UK, international students contributed Â£26 billion to the UK economy in 2018-19, supporting over 200,000 jobs. This includes not only tuition fees, but also spending on accommodation, food, and other goods and services.

In addition to their economic contributions, international students also bring a diverse array of skills and perspectives to the UK workforce. They are more likely to start their own businesses, creating jobs and driving innovation. And as they become integrated into UK society, they also become valuable cultural ambassadors, strengthening ties between the UK and their home countries.

But perhaps most importantly, international students help to foster a more open and inclusive society. They bring with them new ideas and ways of thinking, challenging us to see the world in different ways and to be more tolerant and understanding of different cultures.

As the UK continues to navigate the challenges of Brexit, it is more important than ever to look to the future and to build bridges with the rest of the world. And as Vice Chancellor of the University of Anywhere, I strongly believe that international students are a vital part of that future. They bring economic, social, and cultural benefits to the UK, and we should do all that we can to welcome them and support them in their studies and beyond.

At the University of Anywhere, we will continue to support our international students and to promote the value that they bring to our university and to the UK as a whole. We believe that by working together, we can build a brighter future for everyone.
